# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Recruiters, hiring managers, collaborators, peers, and juniors who want to understand Abhishek Muralidharan's technical range, shipped projects, and working style. They come to assess whether he can contribute to applied AI, backend engineering, and product-minded technical teams.

## Product Purpose

A portfolio that communicates Abhishek's technical depth, shipped projects, and leadership experience as a CS undergrad building applied AI and backend systems. Success means a visitor quickly understands what he builds, why the work is credible, and where he could contribute next.

## Brand Personality

Calm - Competent - Meticulous

Quiet confidence, not self-promotion. Precise and technical without being cold. Every detail earns its place. The interface feels considered but never precious: it is a workshop, not a gallery.

## Anti-references

Not generic template portfolios: the usual three-card developer grid, standard tech-stack icon wall, or centered hero with an avatar. Not over-designed or artsy; never style over substance or readability. No AI-purple gradients, no fake dashboards, no hand-drawn SVG illustrations, no loud animations. The right balance is distinctive but easy to scan.

## Design Principles

1. **Substance first.** Show the work, the architecture, the scale. Design frames the content; it never substitutes for it.
2. **Confidence through restraint.** One accent color, one display moment per section, minimal motion. The page trusts the reader to stay.
3. **Technical sincerity.** The craft of the site should reflect the craft of the builder. Clean code, clean layout, no shortcuts.
4. **Calm hierarchy.** Each section has one job. Headlines are short. Eyebrows are rare. The reader moves at their own pace.
5. **Ship for everyone.** Accessible contrast, reduced-motion support, dark + light mode. Good design includes everyone.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA target. Reduced motion via `prefers-reduced-motion: reduce`. Dark mode as primary with light mode support via `prefers-color-scheme`. Keyboard navigable. Semantic HTML.
